In-app purchases

Yamadori Pro is a one-time purchase handled by Google Play Billing. Transactions are processed by Google; Yamadori does not collect or store your payment details. Only a purchase token is kept locally on the device to enable Pro features.

Your rights

Yamadori does not create user accounts and does not send your scouting data to a publisher server. You can view, edit and delete your data directly in the app (trees, photos, voice notes, caches). For any question, contact us at the address above.

Yamadori Manifesto

Yamadori does not sync your scouting entries to the cloud. Your field notebook lives in your pocket.

On your device

Your notebook, your phone

No account, no Yamadori server. Your scouting entries, photos, voice notes and starting point stay on this device. By default, data is stored unencrypted in local memory; you can enable local encryption in Settings (optional, Android) — it protects trees, parking, export password and GPS settings, but not weather or map caches.

Built for the forest

Map, weather and cadastre data are cached locally. GPS works without a network — even deep in the woods.

Protect your notebook

Export a backup regularly (USB drive, personal cloud). Without an export password, the file only ensures integrity (checksums) — not confidentiality: anyone with the file can extract the data. With a password, the entire archive is encrypted (PBKDF2 + AES-GCM). The hint is a memory aid only; it cannot recover the password.

Full transparency

Like a paper notebook in your bag, your scouting entries remain readable on an unlocked device. Yamadori does not use Android automatic backup to Google. We recommend a screen lock and password-protected exports.

Android permissions

Camera: tree photos only, when you tap Capture. Microphone: voice notes (30 s max), on your action. Location: scouting, map and weather, only while the app is in the foreground. Compass: heading during capture, processed locally. The permissions wizard on first launch guides you — nothing is requested without reason.

Online

Minimal connectivity

When online, only your GPS coordinates are sent to public services for weather, agronomy and cadastre — never your photos or notes. Services used: Open-Meteo, IGN Géoplateforme, Nominatim (OpenStreetMap).

Third-party services consulted

Open-Meteo (forecasts and climate history), IGN Géoplateforme (map tiles), IGN Apicarto (cadastre, public forests, Natura 2000 and ZNIEFF protected areas), Nominatim/OpenStreetMap (place labels). Yamadori has no intermediary server: your coordinates go directly to these public services, each with its own privacy policy. Your photos and notes never leave the device this way.

No tracking

No Yamadori account, no ads, no analytics (Firebase, crash reporters, etc.). No data resale. No third-party cookies: the app runs locally in an Android WebView.

You are in control

You decide who sees your spots

Sharing a tree or exporting a backup is entirely your choice. Nothing is sent to third parties automatically.

Transparent GPS

In the foreground, GPS is used to scout a tree, display the map and contextualise weather. Tracking stops when the app is in the background. No location is sent to a server.

You cut the network

Each network connection can be disabled individually in Settings. If you turn off an API, no more live requests; already downloaded cache remains available offline.

Data inventory

What Yamadori handles for your field notebook — where it is stored, what goes online and how to delete it.

GPS coordinates

Stored on device (trees, parking, position). Sent online: latitude/longitude only, to enabled APIs. Shareable via export or tree sharing. Deletion: delete the tree or clear caches in Settings.

Photos

Stored on device. Never sent online by Yamadori. Shareable via export or tree sharing. Deletion: delete the tree.

Voice notes

Stored on device. Never sent online. Shareable via backup export only. Deletion: delete the tree.

Weather and climate

Cached locally after a request. Coordinates sent to Open-Meteo when that API is enabled. Not shared. Deletion: Settings → clear weather cache.

IGN map tiles

Cached locally. Tile requests for the visible map area when the map API is enabled. Not shared. Deletion: Settings → clear map cache.

Cadastre and protected areas

Cached locally. Coordinates sent to IGN when those APIs are enabled. Not shared. Deletion: Settings → clear cadastre cache.
